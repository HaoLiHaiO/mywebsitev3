Front-of-the-front-end

I am a proficient front-of-the-front-end developer. Never heard of that term? Check out this great article, called front-of-the-front-end and back-of-the-front-end web development, by Brad Frost. I love working with designers and converting their designs, sketches, mockups and drawings with HTML markup, CSS and Javascript (JS) to code accessible, fully responsive and SEO-friendly websites from scratch.

I am also obsessed with getting the highest Lighthouse scores. Of course, we have to be careful, having a perfect lighthouse score doesn't mean everything is perfect. It's even possible to get perfect Lighthouse scores with completely inacessible and non-optimized websites as Manuel Matuzovic showed in an article in 2019.

Back-of-the-front-end

As a front end developer, I also like back-of-the-front-end development. For example, seeing everything that is possible with APIs is something that fascinates me. Being able to work with data from NASA or Microsoft is an absolutely delightful experience. Want to know what time it is on Mars, what the temperature is over there or just see a random picture related to space exploration? Easy and fun with NASA's APIs! Handling things like CRUD functionality or controlling application states are things I do.

Translation

I'm a technical translator. I've been working as a freelance translator, editor and proofreader for over ten years. I translate from Chinese, English, French and German into French and English. I have three master's degrees, including one in linguistics.

“Without translation, I would be limited to the borders of my own country. The translator is my most important ally. He introduces me to the world.” – Italo Calvino

Being bilingual doesn't mean being a good translator. A translator needs to understand the technical aspects and all related linguistic concepts. Attention to details, a deep knowledge of the topic and precision are essential skills if one wants to convey a message as accurately as possible. An advanced understanding of a language and its mechanisms are what enables a translator to create nuanced and meaningful translations.
